Privacy & Security Policy

1. Introduction

Our company recognizes that the protection of personal data is a fundamental right of every user. This Privacy and Security Policy explains what data we collect, how we process it, what it is used for, and what measures we take to protect it. We strive to ensure users‚Äô full trust in our services and implement international security standards, including the GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation), the CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act), as well as national requirements applicable in the jurisdictions where we operate.

2. Terms and Definitions

For the purposes of this Policy, the following definitions apply: Personal Data ‚Äî any information that directly or indirectly allows the identification of a user, including name, contact details, residential address, payment data, and behavioral information. Data Processing ‚Äî any action related to personal data: collection, recording, systematization, storage, updating, use, transfer, anonymization, or deletion. Cookies ‚Äî small text files stored on the user‚Äôs device that allow us to recognize their browser upon repeat visits and to retain settings and enhance personalization. Anonymization ‚Äî actions that exclude the possibility of identifying a user without using additional information.

3. Principles of Data Processing

We follow strict principles based on international information security standards: Lawfulness and Transparency ‚Äî we collect and process data only when there is a legal basis and inform users of the purposes; Data Minimization ‚Äî we collect only what is necessary; Accuracy and Relevance ‚Äî data must be up to date; Storage Limitation ‚Äî data is not stored longer than needed; Security and Confidentiality ‚Äî we apply technical and organizational measures to protect data from leaks and unauthorized access.

4. Categories of Data Collected

We collect the following categories of information: Identification Data (first name, last name, date of birth, gender); Contact Data (email, phone, delivery and billing address); Financial Information (card data via payment gateways, transactions, invoices ‚Äî we do not store full card details); Technical Data (IP address, cookies, device model, browser type); Geolocation (with consent for delivery optimization and personalization); Purchase History (cart items, orders, returns); Communications (support correspondence, reviews, service requests).

5. Purposes of Data Processing

Data is collected and processed solely for specific, transparent, and legitimate purposes: Order Fulfillment (registration, purchase processing, delivery, returns); Customer Support (responding to requests, resolving issues); Financial Settlements (payments, refunds, invoicing); Marketing (personalized offers, discount and promotion notifications); Service Improvement (behavior analysis, feature testing); Legal Obligations (retention under tax and other laws).

6. Legal Bases for Processing

We process personal data only when a legal basis exists: User Consent (e.g., newsletters or cookies); Contract Performance (orders, delivery, payment); Legal Obligations (retaining financial records); Legitimate Interests (fraud prevention, service security) provided these do not override user rights.

7. Data Retention Periods

We retain data only for as long as necessary: Transaction Data ‚Äî 5 years (tax requirements); Account Data ‚Äî until the account is deleted; Support Correspondence ‚Äî up to 3 years to improve service quality. After retention ends, data is deleted or anonymized.

8. Technical Security Measures

We apply SSL/TLS encryption for data in transit; strong algorithms like AES-256 and bcrypt/Argon2 for stored secrets; regular vulnerability testing and penetration tests; comprehensive system logging; and multi-layered access controls for staff.

9. Administrative Security Measures

Organizational procedures complement technology: access is granted only to staff who need it; role-based access ensures managers see only necessary data while administrators have broader rights; staff receive regular security training; and internal audits verify adherence to this Policy.

10. Physical Protection

Data is hosted in professional data centers with biometric and card-based access control, 24/7 video surveillance, fire suppression, backup power, and climate systems. Server-room access is strictly limited and fully logged.

11. Payment Data

We do not store full card details on our servers. Transactions are processed by PCI DSS‚Äìcompliant providers. Tokenization replaces real card data with unique identifiers, reducing the risk of leaks and fraud. We record only what is necessary for accounting and tax purposes.

12. Cookies and Similar Technologies

We use cookies and similar storage (LocalStorage, SessionStorage, pixels) to ensure correct site operation, save preferences, simplify sign-in, personalize offers, and analyze statistics. Cookies include functional, analytical, and advertising types. Users can disable cookies in the browser, which may affect some features.

13. Analytics and Monitoring

We use tools such as Google Analytics, Meta Pixel, and Yandex.Metrica to understand site usage and improve our services. Data is analyzed in anonymized, aggregated form and not used to identify individuals.

14. Personalized Offers

Machine learning analyzes purchase history, views, and interests to generate recommendations, promotions, and discounts. Personalization aims to enhance convenience and can be disabled in account settings.

15. Fraud Protection

Multi-layer anti-fraud measures analyze transactions in real time, detect suspicious activity, and may temporarily suspend operations for verification. IP and geolocation monitoring helps flag unusual logins; we may contact customers for additional checks.

16. User Rights

We uphold: Access (know what data is held and how it is used); Rectification; Erasure (where permitted); Restriction; Portability; and Objection (e.g., to marketing or analytics). Requests are handled per applicable law.

17. Account Management

Users can update personal details, addresses, language/currency preferences, and passwords; enable 2FA; or delete their account. Upon deletion, data is removed or anonymized except where law requires retention (e.g., tax records).

18. Marketing Communications

Marketing messages (promotions, discounts, personalized offers) are sent only with consent. Every message includes an opt-out link. Users control subscriptions and can opt out at any time.

19. Push Notifications

Mobile app users may opt in to push notifications for order statuses, offers, and news. Notifications can be disabled in device or app settings and never include sensitive data like passwords or card details.

20. Geolocation

With consent, location data is used to improve service‚Äîdelivery estimates, nearest pickup points, regional promotions. Such data is kept no longer than necessary and not shared without consent.

21. Logging and Monitoring

We log significant events (sign-ins, account changes, orders, failed logins) for security and operational analysis. Access to logs is restricted to authorized personnel. Logs are retained for defined periods and then archived or anonymized.

22. Security Incidents

We follow a defined incident response protocol for unauthorized access, leaks, or data alteration. Where required, we notify affected users within 72 hours of discovery (GDPR). Post-incident, we implement additional safeguards to reduce recurrence risk.

23. Use of APIs

API access uses authenticated, authorized requests over HTTPS/TLS 1.3 with unique API keys and rate limits. Only vetted partners receive access, limited to agreed scopes. Activity is monitored to prevent abuse.

24. Social Networks and Third-Party Services

Third-party login (e.g., Google, Facebook, Apple ID) provides only minimal data (name, email, unique ID). We do not access passwords. Usage is governed by third-party policies; users can limit permissions granted.

25. Processing of Photos and Documents

Where users upload images or documents (e.g., for identity verification), they are used solely for verification and stored encrypted with strictly limited access. After verification, materials are deleted or archived with restricted access.

26. Automation and AI

We use AI to personalize content and streamline support. Decisions with potential legal effect (e.g., account blocking) are never fully automated; a human reviews such cases.

27. Judicial and Legal Requests

We may disclose personal data only as required by law (court, law enforcement, regulators). Requests are validated, and only the minimum necessary data is provided. Where lawful, users are informed of such disclosures.

28. Returns and Disputes

To process returns, exchanges, and disputes, we use order details, contact data, and support history solely to resolve the issue. After completion, data is kept for a limited time and then deleted or anonymized.

29. Data Localization and Multilingual Support

Data may be stored and processed on servers in different countries depending on user location and legal requirements, with protection levels aligned to international standards. In case of discrepancies, the Policy version in the operating jurisdiction‚Äôs language prevails.

30. User Responsibilities

Users must keep passwords secret, use strong combinations, and avoid sharing access. If an account is compromised due to user actions, the company is not liable for resulting damage, though we will assist with recovery.

31. Company Responsibility

We take all reasonable and technically feasible measures to protect personal data, investing in encryption, monitoring, and protective technologies. While we minimize incident impact, absolute security cannot be guaranteed given inherent internet risks.

32. Employee Training and Certification

All employees undergo mandatory security and privacy training, covering international standards and confidential data handling. Annual certification is required; non-passing staff are retrained to ensure only qualified specialists may access data.

33. Interaction with Government Authorities

We cooperate with authorities strictly under applicable law. Disclosures occur only upon official, legally compliant requests, and we provide the minimum required data. Where lawful, users are informed of disclosures.

34. Partner Services

We work with delivery, payments, and analytics partners that pass security due diligence. Contracts include NDAs and data protection clauses. We collaborate only with entities providing security at or above our standards.

35. Limitation of Liability

We are not responsible for the privacy practices of third-party sites linked from our services. Users should review those policies and exercise caution when sharing data, as we control only our systems and processes.

36. Changes and Updates

We may update this Policy to reflect changes in law, business processes, or technology. Updated versions are published with the latest revision date and may be communicated via email or push notifications.

37. Log Retention Policy

Activity logs are stored in secure systems that prevent unauthorized alteration or deletion. Retention follows internal and legal requirements; afterward, logs are archived or anonymized. Access is limited to security personnel.

38. VPN and Remote Access

Remote access is allowed only through secure VPN channels using TLS 1.3. Public Wi‚ÄëFi may not be used for confidential work without VPN. Only corporate or pre-approved, security-compliant devices are permitted.

39. Employee Access and Roles

We enforce least-privilege access via role-based models (managers, support, admins). All actions are audited. Access rights are periodically reviewed and revoked automatically upon role change or termination.

40. Employee Mobile Devices

Under our BYOD policy, devices must have up-to-date software, antivirus, and encryption. Risky third-party apps require approval. If a device is lost, corporate data can be remotely wiped.

41. Network Protections

We deploy NGFWs, IDS/IPS, and application-layer filtering with segmented networks that separate user data from admin systems. Real-time monitoring blocks suspicious connections automatically.

42. DDoS Protection

Cloud scrubbing services (e.g., Cloudflare, AWS Shield) analyze traffic patterns to mitigate DDoS, blocking suspicious IPs or regions to keep the site stable under hostile load.

43. Protection Against Malware

Servers and workstations run modern, proactively updated anti-malware with behavioral analysis and centralized monitoring to detect and block suspicious files or processes.

44. Bug Bounty Program

We encourage external researchers to responsibly disclose vulnerabilities. Submissions are validated, prioritized by severity, and remediated promptly, with rewards aligned to impact.

45. Data Backups

Critical data is backed up on schedule and stored encrypted in separate infrastructure and regions. Regular restore drills ensure rapid recovery and minimal user impact during incidents.

46. Database Encryption

Personal data in databases is encrypted with AES‚Äë256 at rest and TLS in transit. Keys are stored in HSMs with strict, MFA‚Äëgated access and segregated duties.

47. Code Change Control

We use version control (e.g., Git) with mandatory code review and security testing for critical modules. Repository access is restricted and issued via personal access keys.

48. Vendor and Contractor Control

Suppliers undergo security due diligence before onboarding. Contracts mandate confidentiality and data protection; periodic reassessments ensure ongoing compliance.

49. DevOps and CI/CD Policy

Automated CI/CD pipelines run correctness and security tests to reduce human error. All deployments are logged and can be rolled back if needed.

50. Vulnerability Management

Automated and manual scans identify vulnerabilities. Findings are categorized and remediated on defined timelines‚Äîcritical issues immediately, others in planned cycles. A registry tracks resolution and recurrence prevention.

51. Vulnerability Management Plan

Each threat is logged, risk‚Äërated (low to critical), assigned to an owner, and fixed per SLA timelines. Documentation enables retrospective analysis and continuous improvement.

52. Disaster Recovery Plan

Our DR plan covers large‚Äëscale failures and crises with encrypted backups, regional failover, and prioritized system restoration. Regular simulations validate readiness.

53. Business Continuity

BCP measures safeguard key processes via redundancy, backup sites, and load distribution. Annual tests and updates align with evolving business and infrastructure.

54. Use of Cloud Services

We use ISO/IEC 27001 and PCI DSS‚Äìaligned cloud providers (e.g., AWS/Azure/GCP). Data is encrypted; least‚Äëprivilege access and permission reviews are enforced.

55. Security Incident Management

A dedicated team triages incidents by severity, executes containment and remediation, and completes root‚Äëcause analyses. Lessons learned feed policy and control updates.

56. Risk Management

We maintain a living risk matrix to evaluate likelihood and impact, driving mitigation via new controls, process changes, and staff training. Risk management is continuous and embedded.

57. Compliance with International Standards

Our program aligns with ISO/IEC 27001, PCI DSS, GDPR, and CCPA, reinforcing trust with customers and partners.

58. Audits and Assessments

Internal and external audits assess security posture. Findings generate tracked improvement plans overseen by leadership to ensure closure.

59. Customer Access Control

Accounts support strong passwords and optional 2FA. Sessions expire after inactivity; unusual logins may require additional verification.

60. API Key Usage Policy

Unique API keys are issued to vetted partners, time‚Äëlimited, and rotated regularly. Scopes restrict capabilities. Compromised keys are revoked immediately.

61. Email Security Policy

We employ SPF, DKIM, and DMARC to prevent spoofing; advanced spam and phishing filters protect users. We advise verifying domains and avoiding suspicious links.

62. Domain and Certificate Policy

Domains are protected with DNSSEC. All sites use HTTPS with modern ciphers and proactively renewed certificates. Configurations are routinely reviewed.

63. Big Data Processing

We analyze large datasets using anonymization so individuals cannot be identified. Insights drive UX improvements, logistics optimization, feature development, and demand forecasting.

64. Cloud Integration Policy

Cloud integrations require prior security assessment, tokenized access, exhaustive logging, and least‚Äëprivilege scopes. Suspicious activity triggers immediate blocks.

65. Regular Security Scanning

Scheduled automated scans and manual reviews cover servers, databases, and applications. Reports are triaged and remediations tracked to completion.

66. Software Update Policy

Servers and endpoints are kept current. Security patches receive priority and are tested then deployed promptly. Legacy software is prohibited.

67. Log Access Policy

Log data is confidential. Access is time‚Äëboxed via temporary tokens for authorized admins only. All access and changes are themselves logged.

68. Internal Network Policy

We operate on a Zero Trust model with authentication for every connection, segmented via VLANs. Monitoring detects and blocks anomalous activity.

69. Backup Data Center Policy

Independent geographic regions host hot standbys. Real‚Äëtime data sync enables automatic failover. Regular switchover tests validate availability.

70. Cryptographic Key Storage Policy

Keys reside in HSMs, are rotated regularly, and accessed via MFA by a limited set of admins. Compromised keys are revoked and replaced immediately.

71. Software Testing Policy

Releases pass functional, load, and security testing. External penetration tests supplement internal assurance before production deployment.

72. IoT Device Usage Policy

Only certified devices are allowed, isolated on dedicated network segments, and updated routinely. Unsupported or non‚Äëencrypted devices are prohibited.

73. Customer Data Handling Policy

We collect only what‚Äôs necessary for service delivery. Analytics rely on anonymization; marketing use requires explicit consent.

74. Mobile App Security Policy

Apps use TLS 1.3, root/jailbreak detection, and anti‚Äëspoofing measures. We monitor app stores and act against counterfeit apps.

75. Customer and Partner Audit Policy

Partners and enterprise customers may be audited for security compliance. Non‚Äëconformities must be remediated to maintain integration.
